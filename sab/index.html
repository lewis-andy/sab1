Welcome to Nairobi National Park

Experience the wonder of African wildlife just minutes from downtown Nairobi. Established in 1946, Nairobi National Park is Kenya's oldest national park and offers a unique opportunity to view diverse wildlife against the backdrop of the city skyline.

Despite its relatively small size (117 square kilometers), the park boasts an impressive variety of wildlife and is a crucial sanctuary for endangered black rhinos, lions, leopards, cheetahs, hyenas, buffaloes, giraffes, and diverse bird species.

Plan Your Visit

  1. Check current entry fees and park hours on the Kenya Wildlife Service website
  2. Book accommodation nearby or plan for a day trip
  3. Consider hiring a knowledgeable guide for the best experience
  4. Bring binoculars, camera, water, and sun protection
  5. Respect wildlife by maintaining a safe distance and following park regulations

About The Park

Nairobi National Park is a unique ecosystem that supports a rich biodiversity despite its proximity to Kenya's capital city. The park's landscape is characterized by open grass plains, scattered acacia bushes, and forest areas along the bank of the Embakasi River.

Key Features

  • Only protected wildlife sanctuary in the world bordering a capital city
  • Home to over 100 mammal species and 400+ bird species
  • Important breeding ground for the endangered black rhino
  • Diverse ecosystems including forests, plains, and rivers
  • Ivory Burning Site Monument commemorating Kenya's commitment to ending ivory trade
  • Animal orphanage housing and rehabilitating injured and orphaned wildlife

The park plays a crucial role in conservation efforts and provides an important wildlife corridor connected to the Athi-Kapiti plains and Kitengela conservation area. These migration corridors are vital for the long-term survival of the park's wildlife populations.
