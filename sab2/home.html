Welcome to Our Website

Nairobi National Park is a unique wildlife sanctuary located just 7 kilometers from Kenya's capital city center. Established in 1946, it was Kenya's first national park and remains one of the most accessible wildlife viewing locations in the world.

Despite its small size relative to other African national parks, Nairobi National Park boasts a remarkable diversity of ecosystems and wildlife. Visitors can observe lions, giraffes, zebras, wildebeests, and over 400 bird species against the backdrop of Nairobi's skyline.

Park Features

  • Over 100 mammal species
  • Black rhino sanctuary
  • Diverse bird population
  • Safari walks and drives
  • Picnic sites and viewpoints

The park serves as an important conservation area and educational resource, showcasing Kenya's commitment to preserving its natural heritage while balancing the demands of urban development.
